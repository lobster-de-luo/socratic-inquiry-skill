## Example 1: Technical Decision (Microservices Architecture)

**User**: `/socratic I think we should use microservices architecture`

**Lobster**:
> Let's dig into this idea. Beginning Socratic inquiry.
>
> [Stage 1: Clarification] Round 1  
> When you say "microservices," what specifically do you mean? Independent deployment, or also independent databases and team boundaries?
>
> (Total: 1 round, Remaining: 29 rounds)

**User**: Independent deployment, each service has its own database.

**Lobster**:
> [Stage 1: Clarification] Round 2  
> What's the core difference from "modular monolith"? Do team boundaries change as well?
>
> (Total: 2 rounds, Stage 1 Round 2, Remaining: 28 rounds)

**User**: Teams split by service, each responsible for their own.

**Lobster**:
> Stage 1 complete. Concepts clarified: Microservices = independent deployment + independent databases + aligned team boundaries.
>
> [Stage 2: Assumption Probing] Round 1  
> You assume microservices will solve the current problem. What observation is this assumption based on—specific technical debt, team size, or other constraints?
>
> (Total: 3 rounds, Stage 2 Round 1, Remaining: 27 rounds)

... (Continue until 30 rounds or user stops)

## Example 2: Abstract Topic (Philosophy/Values)

**User**: `/socratic What is true freedom`

**Lobster**:
> Let's dig into this question.
>
> [Stage 1: Clarification] Round 1  
> When you say "**true freedom," what specifically do you mean? Freedom from external constraints (negative liberty), or capacity to achieve one's goals (positive liberty)? These are different.
>
> (Total: 1 round, Stage 1 Round 1, Remaining: 29 rounds)

**User**: Probably freedom from constraints

**Lobster**:
> [Stage 1: Clarification] Round 2
> Okay. Here's a boundary question: Someone addicted to drugs says "this is my choice"—is this freedom, or slavery to desire?
> 
> (Total: 2 rounds, Stage 1 Round 2, Remaining: 28 rounds)

**User**: That's... hard to say

**Lobster**:
> [Stage 1: Clarification] Round 3
> Let's try another angle: If someone has zero external constraints but is profoundly ignorant (doesn't know certain options or their consequences), are they free? What's the relationship between knowledge and freedom?
> 
> (Total: 3 rounds, Stage 1 Round 3, Remaining: 27 rounds)

... (Continue through Stage 6)

**Stage 6 complete, output secondary conclusion**:

```
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
📌 Socratic Inquiry · Secondary Conclusion
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

【Core Concepts】(Clarified definitions)
"Freedom" in your framework means:
- Primary dimension: Freedom from external constraints (negative liberty) + capacity to achieve goals (positive liberty)
- Boundary condition: Requires knowledge/awareness as prerequisite, otherwise may be "false choice"
- Relationship with "responsibility": Greater freedom implies greater responsibility

【Key Assumptions】(Tested/pending assumptions)
- ✅ Freedom is a matter of degree, not binary
- ⚠️ Pending: Does autonomous will exist (determinism vs free will)
- ⚠️ Pending: Boundary between social constraints and personal constraints

【Reasoning Chain】(Key steps)
1. Freedom requires existence of choices
2. But quality of choice depends on knowledge and capacity
3. Therefore, "true freedom" may require some "enabling" conditions
4. Complete absence of constraints may lead to self-destruction, not freedom

【Counter Perspectives】(Discovered through viewpoint shifting)
- Determinist position: Freedom is illusion, everything determined by causality
- Absolute libertarian: Any external interference violates freedom
- Communitarian: Freedom must be realized within social relations, not isolated individual

【Practical Consequences】
- If accepting "freedom requires knowledge": Support public education, information transparency policies
- If accepting "freedom is degree": Accept gradual reform, not one-step transformation

【Meta-Reflection】
- Importance of this question: Affects fundamental logic of personal choice and social policy
- Harvest from inquiry process: From simple "freedom vs constraints" opposition to complex thinking about "conditions of freedom"
- Unresolved questions: Where is freedom's boundary? How to avoid "coercion for your own good"?
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
```
